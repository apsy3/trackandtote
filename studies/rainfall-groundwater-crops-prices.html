Research blueprint 01

Rainfall, groundwater, crops, and prices.

A careful first study for asking whether water stress appears later in crop output, mandi arrivals, and price behavior. The aim is evidence with limits, not a quick causal story.

01 Rainfall anomaly actual vs normal, dry spells, extreme days
02 Water buffer groundwater depth, recharge, seasonal recovery
03 Crop response area, production, yield, crop calendar
04 Market signal arrivals, modal price, volatility, spikes

Core question

When rainfall shifts and groundwater is stressed, what later changes can we observe in crop yield, mandi arrivals, and prices?

The working unit is district by month by crop. This lets daily rainfall and mandi data be summarized without losing the agricultural season, and it gives groundwater and crop statistics a common comparison level.

Scientific method

Move from hypothesis to evidence.

  1. Hypothesis

    Write expected relationships before plotting: rainfall deficit during sensitive crop months may reduce yield or arrivals later.

  2. Source audit

    Record provider, license, update date, geography level, frequency, missing fields, and collection method.

  3. Harmonize

    Normalize district names, crop names, dates, units, and market identifiers before joining datasets.

  4. Explore

    Use anomaly maps, time series, seasonal windows, scatter plots, and lag correlation checks.

  5. Test

    Start with lagged correlations, then consider panel regression with district and month controls once the data is stable.

  6. Conclude carefully

    Separate observed association from causation, and publish the limitations beside the chart.

Parameters

What each dataset should contribute.

Rainfall

Actual rainfall, normal rainfall, departure %, dry spell length, rainy days, heavy rainfall days, monsoon window.

Derived signals

Monthly anomaly, crop-stage rainfall, rolling deficit, extreme rainfall flag.

Groundwater

Depth below ground level, pre-monsoon level, post-monsoon recovery, year-on-year change, stress category where available.

Derived signals

Seasonal recharge, long-term decline, drought buffer index.

Crop

Crop, season, district, sown area, production, yield, irrigation share, crop calendar, major producing districts.

Derived signals

Yield anomaly, area response, sensitive growth-stage window.

Market

Mandi, arrivals, modal price, min/max price, monthly average, price spread, volatility, price spike events.

Derived signals

Arrival shock, price spike flag, lag from crop stress to market movement.

Pilot choice

Start narrow: Maharashtra and onion.

Onion is a useful first crop because prices are visible to the public, Maharashtra is a major producing state, and water stress can be investigated through rainfall, groundwater, production, arrivals, and price movement.

Guardrails

What not to claim too early.

Do say

Rainfall deficit was followed by lower arrivals in these markets during this window.

Do not say yet

Rainfall deficit caused the price increase.

Do control for

Storage, transport, exports, imports, MSP, inflation, nearby-state production, and policy events.
