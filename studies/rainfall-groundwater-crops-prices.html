Study 01 | Maharashtra onion markets

Can water stress help explain onion price shocks?

Onion prices are not just a market chart. They are a public signal people notice at home. This field note begins with a careful question: when rainfall is weak and groundwater is stressed, what later changes can we observe in onion output and mandi prices?

Onion prices rise in ways people feel before they read a report. A shortage at a mandi, a sudden price jump, or a bad rainfall season can all become part of the same public conversation. The difficult part is knowing which signals actually belong together.

This study treats water stress as one possible explanation, not the whole story. Prices also move because of storage, transport, exports, imports, policy decisions, demand, and production in nearby regions. The goal is to show the evidence chain plainly enough for general readers, while keeping the method disciplined enough for data work.

What we are testing

If rains are weak during crop-sensitive months, farmers may depend more on groundwater. If groundwater is already deep or slow to recover, crop output can become more fragile. Later, markets may receive less supply, and prices may react. That is the hypothesis. It is not yet proof.

Method

Readable, but not loose.

The method starts simple: define the question, collect public sources, align them to the same geography and time unit, then test whether the signal survives basic checks.

  1. Ask first. Write the hypothesis before looking for a chart.
  2. Audit sources. Record provider, license, update date, missing values, and limits.
  3. Build one table. Keep raw and derived variables separate.
  4. Check lags. Compare one-month, three-month, and six-month relationships.
  5. Publish caveats. Put uncertainty beside the figure, not in a footnote no one sees.

Result language

Can say early: these signals appear together in these districts and months.

Needs testing: the relationship survives seasonality, nearby supply, and policy context.

Do not say yet: water stress caused the price increase.
