Public-data storytelling fails when single observations are presented as full truth.
In India ESG/GHG/OHSE work, we often mix datasets with very different update cycles.

Air-quality streams can be near real-time in some cities.  
Grid factors, annual emissions, and many OHSE indicators are slower and versioned.

That is why each graph in this observatory includes:

- source
- frequency
- latest observation date
- retrieved-at timestamp
- confidence
- status

In Step 1, all series are mock data by design. This gives us a safe way to finalize chart
contracts and metadata rules before live connectors are introduced.

The next phase is not "more charts first." The next phase is better governance:

1. Connector-level source checks
2. Snapshot caching
3. Transparent stale/fresh labels
4. Clear caveats before interpretation

The calculator comes later. First, we make sure the public evidence layer is readable,
traceable, and honest about what it can and cannot claim.
