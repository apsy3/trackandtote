Public data becomes reporting-ready only when the context travels with the numbers.

That means each series needs more than values:

- source and source URL
- update frequency
- latest observation date
- retrieval timestamp
- known limitations

Without those fields, teams accidentally compare datasets that move on different clocks and
overstate what can be claimed.

For Track & Tote projects, the practical test is simple:

1. Can a reader trace where the data came from?
2. Can they tell how fresh it is?
3. Can they see what uncertainty remains?

If the answer is no, the visual is still a draft, not evidence.
